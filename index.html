Present Perfect Progressive

The "I Have Been Waiting" Tense

üìñ What is it?

Used for actions that started in the past and are still happening now.

(Digunakan untuk kegiatan yang dimulai di masa lalu dan masih berlangsung sampai sekarang)

üî¢ Formula:

Subject + have/has + been + verb-ing

‚Ä¢ I/You/We/They + have been waiting

‚Ä¢ He/She/It + has been waiting

üí° Examples:

"I have been waiting at the departure gate for 2 hours."

"Aku sudah menunggu di gerbang keberangkatan selama 2 jam (dan masih menunggu)."

have been waiting for 2 hours (duration)

"She has been studying English since 2020."

"Dia sudah belajar bahasa Inggris sejak tahun 2020 (dan masih belajar)."

has been studying since 2020 (starting point)

"We have been standing in line since 9 AM."

"Kami sudah berdiri antri sejak jam 9 pagi (dan masih berdiri)."

have been standing since 9 AM

FOR

Use for duration (how long)

(Digunakan untuk durasi/lama waktu)

  • ‚Ä¢ for 2 hours (selama 2 jam)
  • ‚Ä¢ for 3 days (selama 3 hari)
  • ‚Ä¢ for 10 minutes (selama 10 menit)
  • ‚Ä¢ for a long time (selama waktu yang lama)

SINCE

Use for starting point (when it began)

(Digunakan untuk titik awal/waktu mulai)

  • ‚Ä¢ since 2020 (sejak 2020)
  • ‚Ä¢ since morning (sejak pagi)
  • ‚Ä¢ since 9 AM (sejak jam 9)
  • ‚Ä¢ since last week (sejak minggu lalu)

‚ú® JUST (Baru Saja)

Use for something that happened a very short time ago.

(Digunakan untuk sesuatu yang terjadi baru saja/moment yang lalu)

"I have just arrived at the airport."

"Saya baru saja tiba di bandara."

‚úÖ ALREADY (Sudah)

Use for something that happened before now (earlier than expected).

(Digunakan untuk sesuatu yang sudah terjadi sebelum waktu yang diharapkan)

"I have already checked in."

"Saya sudah check-in."

‚ùì YET (Belum)

Use in negative sentences and questions. Usually at the END of the sentence.

(Digunakan di kalimat negatif dan pertanyaan, biasanya di akhir kalimat)

"Have you arrived yet?"

"Apakah kamu sudah tiba belum?"

"I haven't found my passport yet."

"Saya belum menemukan paspor saya."

‚è≥ STILL (Masih)

Use for something that is continuing (not finished).

(Digunakan untuk sesuatu yang masih berlangsung/belum selesai)

"I am still waiting at the gate."

"Saya masih menunggu di gerbang."

Cheat Sheet (Contekan)

Present Perfect Progressive:

S + have/has + been + V-ing

I have been waiting for 2 hours.

FOR vs SINCE:

FOR + duration (2 hours, 3 days)

SINCE + starting point (2020, 9 AM)

JUST/ALREADY/YET/STILL:

JUST = baru saja | ALREADY = sudah

YET = belum (di akhir) | STILL = masih
